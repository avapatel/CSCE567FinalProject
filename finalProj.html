Introduction

The Washington Commanders have been in a rut for quite some time. That was until Heisman Trophy recipient Jayden Daniels signed to the Washington Commanders. This project explores key stats comparing the Washington Commanders pre and post Jayden Daniels.

Washington Commanders Career Passing Leaders

This visualization is an interactive bar graph. In all of Commanders/Redskins history, these are the career passing leaders. Keep in mind that the majority of these players have been with the Commanders for multiple seasons. For Jayden Daniels to be 21st out of the 150+ players listed is amazing. It just shows how insanely good Daniels is. You can use the sliding bar on the right of the graph to filter players by number of seasons played.

Daniels VS Howell: Simple Stats

Sam Howell was the starting quarterback in the 2023-2024 season where the Commanders went 4-13. Jayden Daniels was the starting quarterback in the 2024-2025 season where the Commanders went 12-5, an insane one year turnaround. Daniels (24) and Howell (23) both played and started in 17 games in their respective seasons. Jayden Daniels attempted 480 passes and completed 331 whereas Howell attempted a whopping 612 passes but only completed 388. Daniels had a higher success rate (47%) and more yards per attempt (10.8 vs 10.2). Howell was sacked 65 times compared to Daniels' 47, and Howell threw 21 interceptions to Daniels' 9. Daniels also had more passing touchdowns (25 vs 21).
