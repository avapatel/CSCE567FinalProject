Was Jayden Daniels a Game Changer?

Ava Patel

Introduction

The Washington Commanders have been in a rut for quite some time. That was until Heisman Trophy recipient Jayden Daniels signed to the Washington Commanders. This project explores key stats comparing the Washington Commanders pre and post Jayden Daniels.

Washington Commanders Career Passing Leaders

This visualization is a interactive bar graph. In all of Commanders/Redskins history, these are the career passing leaders. Keep in mind that the majority of these players have been with the Commanders for multiple seasons. For Jayden Daniels to be 21st out of the 150+ players I have listed is amazing. It just shows how insanely good Daniels is. You can use the sliding bar on the right of the graph to filter players by number of seasons played.

Daniels VS Howell: Simple Stats

Sam Howell was the starting quarterback in the 2023-2024 season where the commanders went 4-13. Jayden Daniels was the starting quarterback in the 2024-2025 season where the commanders went 12-5 which is an insane one year turnaround. Daniels (24) and Howell (23) both played and started in 17 games in their respective seasons. Jayden Daniels attempted a total of 480 passes and completed 331 where Howell attempted a whopping 612 passes and only completed 388 of them. During the 2024-2025 regular season, Daniels had a passing success rate of about 47% and gained on average 10.8 yards per pass. During the 2023-2024 regular season, Daniels had a passing success rate of about 42% and gained on average 10.2 yards per pass. Sam Howell was sacked 65 times where Daniels was sacked only 47 times. In both aspects Daniels overall is better when it comes to not throwing interceptions and passing touchdowns. Howell threw 21 interceptions where Daniels only threw 9. When it came to passing touchdowns, Daniels threw 25 where Howell threw 21.

Wins & Losses 2010-2024

This visualization compares the wins and losses since 2010. As you can see between the 2023-2024 season and the 2024-2025 season, the wins and losses did a complete 360. Before Jayden, the most wins that we had was in 2015, and even then in 2024 we ended up getting an almost record high 12 wins for the season, post Jayden.

Points Scored 2010-2024: Commanders & Opponents

This visualization demonstrates the number of points scored by the Commanders and allowed by the Commanders. Again as we saw in the graph above, the number of points scored and allowed did a complete flip, which is exactly what we want to see.

Margin of Victory

The Margin of Victory is calculated by (points scored-points allowed)/number of games played. The higher the number the better. This shows that over the past 15 years, the Washington Commanders have only had a positive margin of victory 5 times. In 2025 with Daniels as the starting quarterback, the Margin of Victory is 5.5, the highest its been since 1976.
